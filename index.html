About me

Greetings! Saeed here.

I've always felt good with numbers. Having majored in Electrical Engineering at CSULA, and realizing it isn't the route for me, I started exploring options for myself. I moved to Ohio, and transferred to the University of Akron. Realizing I had a long road ahead of me trying to get a bachelor's in Computer Science, I wanted to explore options that would make the conversion to software engineering less tedious for me. I did a bit of researching and lo and behold, I'm a Coding-Temple student.

Why software engineering?

I love challenges. The sweat going down my forehead, stressing, trying to figure out a problem. While working with electrical engineering, I found that the challenges I was dealing with didn't let me explore my creative side. I stumbled upon codes plenty of times in my life, and it always begged the question: "What is all of this?!"

Pondering with all of this, I figured I'd look into it with more detail. To be honest, it started off too basic for me to really be convinced that coding is the tell-all to an app. However I started learning more and more, and the pieces were pulling itself together. Everything was seemingly connected and making sense to me. I love the creativity you can have with software engineering, down from the way a web page can look like, all the way to how the app functions. Everything is accounted for and the precision on codes, makes me excited more than anything.

What led to Coding Temple?

The ideal option

Obviously, coding camps can be fairly pricey and we wanted to get the best bang for our buck. Well with Coding Temple, they had us take this test prior to enrolling that tested our knowledge on computers/code in general. They were generous with their room for error. I felt that teaching myself had always been my forte for learning. With the lectures I've seen so far, I don't feel like I'm in school and there's a bunch of other students dreading being there. It feels like Brandon is a very educated friend and is teaching me how to code in a way that I can understand.
